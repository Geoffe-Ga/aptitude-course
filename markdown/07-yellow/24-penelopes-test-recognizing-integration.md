## Penelope's Test: Recognizing Integration

But there's one more test. Even after Odysseus reveals himself, Penelope doesn't believe it's him. She tests him with a secret: she tells a servant to move their marriage bed.

Odysseus erupts. "That's impossible! I built the bed around a living olive tree. The tree is rooted in the ground. The bed can't be moved without cutting the tree."

And Penelope knows. Only the real Odysseus would know that. Only someone who was *present* for the building of the bed, who integrated that experience into his being, would react that way.

This is Yellow's wisdom: integration is not performative. You can't fake it. You either *know* it in your bones, or you don't.

And when you do—when you've integrated all the stages, all the lessons, all the parts of yourself—people who are also integrated will *recognize* you. They'll see it. They'll know.

That's the homecoming Yellow offers: not return to who you were, but arrival at who you've become.
