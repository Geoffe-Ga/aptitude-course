## The Default Habit of Yellow: Daily Meditation

At Yellow, the habit *is* the practice. **Daily Meditation**—45 minutes, minimum, every day.

This is the discipline that separates dilettantes from practitioners. Anyone can meditate when they feel like it. Yellow asks you to meditate *especially* when you don't.

Because that's when the real training happens. When you're tired, stressed, resistant—and you sit anyway. When the mind is a hurricane and you keep returning to the breath anyway. That's when you build the muscle of Free Will.

The research is clear: daily meditation changes everything. Attention span. Emotional regulation. Stress response. Immune function. Empathy. Decision-making. Sleep quality. Chronic pain. The list goes on.

But beyond the neuroscience, there's the lived experience. When you sit every day, you start to notice patterns you couldn't see before. You catch the loop of rumination before it spirals. You see the moment when the ego hijacks the conversation. You feel the shift from presence to reactivity—and you learn to stay present.

This is what Culadasa means when he says that mindfulness allows us to "recognize our options, choose our responses wisely, and take control over the direction of our lives." It's not magic. It's training.

Yellow asks: Are you willing to train? Are you willing to sit, day after day, even when it's hard, even when you don't see immediate results, even when your mind screams that this is pointless?

Because that's what it takes to become an Intentional Actor. That's what it takes to cultivate Free Will.

45 minutes. Every day. No exceptions.

That's the habit. That's the path.
