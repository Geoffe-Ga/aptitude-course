## Building the Daily Meditation Habit: The First 100 Days

Here's how to actually make this sustainable.

**Days 1-30: Establishing the Container**

The first month is about *consistency over quality*. Don't worry about whether you're "doing it right." Just show up.

Pick a time and stick to it. Morning is ideal (before the day's demands hijack your attention), but any consistent time works. Set an alarm. Treat it as non-negotiable as brushing your teeth.

You'll miss days. That's okay. The practice is: when you miss, don't spiral. Just return the next day. No guilt. No story. Just begin again.

Track it. Use a habit tracker, a calendar, a simple check box. The act of marking the day builds momentum.

**Days 31-60: Working Through Resistance**

By week five, the novelty has worn off. This is when most people quit.

You'll have days where you sit for 10 minutes and your mind is chaos. You'll think, "This isn't working." That thought is part of the process. Notice it. Don't believe it. Keep sitting.

You'll have days where you "don't have time." You do. You have time for what you prioritize. 45 minutes is 3% of your day. You have it.

This is the month where you build *discipline*. Not motivation. Discipline. Motivation is a feeling. Discipline is a structure. When motivation fades (and it will), discipline carries you.

**Days 61-100: Deepening the Practice**

By month three, something shifts. The practice becomes part of your identity. You're not someone who "tries to meditate." You're a meditator.

The sits start to vary. Some are blissful. Some are restless. Some are boring. You stop judging them. You recognize: each sit is data. Each one is training, regardless of how it feels.

This is when you start to notice the *off-the-cushion* effects. You're less reactive in conflict. More present in conversation. You catch yourself before you spiralinto rumination. The training is working.

By day 100, you're not thinking about whether to sit. You're thinking about how to deepen the practice. What technique to explore. What book to read. You've crossed the threshold from beginner to practitioner.
