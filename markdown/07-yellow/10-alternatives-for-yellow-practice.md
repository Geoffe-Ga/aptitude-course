## Alternatives for Yellow Practice

If sitting meditation for 45 minutes isn't accessible—due to physical limitations, trauma history, or neurodivergence—there are other ways to cultivate sustained, integrative attention. The key is that whatever practice you choose must be *at least 45 minutes* and must train both concentration and awareness.

**1. Walking Meditation (45 min)**

Find a quiet path or room where you can walk slowly, continuously, for 45 minutes. Focus on the sensations of each step—the lifting of the foot, the movement through air, the placement on the ground. When your mind wanders, return to the walking.

This is especially good for people who struggle with stillness. It gives the body something to do while the mind trains.

**2. Yoga Practice (45 min)**

A slow, mindful yoga flow—holding each pose for several breaths, moving with complete attention to alignment and sensation—can serve the same function as seated meditation. The key is that it's *not* a workout. It's a moving meditation. You're training attention through the body.

**3. Free-Writing / Stream of Consciousness (45 min)**

Set a timer and write without stopping for 45 minutes. No editing, no crossing out. Just let the hand move. This is a form of awareness training—you're watching the contents of your mind arise on the page. Over time, you begin to see the patterns, the loops, the conditioning. And that seeing is the beginning of choice.

**4. Subtle Energy Scanning (45 min)**

Lie down or sit comfortably. Spend 45 minutes slowly scanning your body for subtle energetic sensations—tingling, warmth, pressure, flow. This is a somatic meditation. You're training interoceptive awareness—the ability to sense what's happening inside the body. And that awareness is the foundation of self-regulation.

**5. Chanting / Kirtan (45 min)**

Repetitive devotional chanting, done with full attention for 45 minutes, is a form of mantra meditation. The repetition stabilizes the mind. The devotion opens the heart. And the sustained focus trains concentration.

**6. Noting Practice (45 min)**

This is a vipassana technique. Sit in meditation and mentally "note" everything that arises: "thinking," "hearing," "itching," "planning," "remembering." The noting keeps you anchored in the present moment and prevents you from getting lost in thought. Do this for 45 minutes and you'll develop a kind of meta-awareness that's astonishing in its clarity.

All of these work. But whatever you choose, commit to it. Do it daily. Give it the full 45 minutes. This is not optional for Yellow. This is the practice that unlocks everything else.
