## What to Track: Building Your Practice Log

Yellow loves data. So track your sits. After each session, jot down:

1. **Duration**: Did you make the full 45 minutes? If not, how long?
2. **Quality of attention**: On a scale of 1-10, how stable was your focus?
3. **Dominant state**: Were you mostly distracted? Concentrated? Spacious?
4. **Somatic markers**: Did you feel pīti? Tension? Restlessness?
5. **Insights**: Did anything click? Any moments of clarity?

Over weeks and months, you'll see patterns. You'll notice that certain times of day work better. That sleep quality affects your sits. That your capacity is building.

This isn't to judge yourself. It's to *see* the system. To recognize that meditation is a skill, and skills develop through consistent practice.
