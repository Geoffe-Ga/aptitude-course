## The Mode of the Wavelength of Yellow: Integrate (Do)

Yellow introduces the fourth Mode: **Integrate**. Where Inhabit (Beige, Purple) was about internal foundation, Express (Red, Blue) was about externalizing, and Collaborate (Orange, Green) was about co-creation, Integrate is about *synthesis*. You're taking all the parts and weaving them into a coherent whole.

But Yellow is **Integrate (Do)**—the masculine, active expression of integration. You're not just passively receiving wholeness (that's Teal). You're *building* it. You're doing the work of connecting the dots, seeing the systems, and acting from a unified center.

This is the Wavelength of the meditator who's logged thousands of hours. The systems thinker who can hold multiple variables in mind at once. The leader who can draw on different approaches depending on context. You're practicing *flexible responsiveness*.

The six phases of Yellow's Wavelength map the territory of focused awareness:

**Rising: Rx = Focus | OD = Hyperfocus**

**Peaking: Rx = Concentration | OD = Obsession**

**Withdrawal: Rx = Adjustment | OD = Stagnation**

**Diminishing: Rx = Reorienting | OD = Dithering**

**Bottoming Out: Rx = Settling | OD = Depression**

**Restoration: Rx = Reset | OD = Addiction**

This is the rhythm of the mind training itself. Of attention becoming a tool you wield consciously rather than a force that moves you unconsciously.

We'll break down each phase in detail. But first, notice: Yellow's Wavelength is *inward*. Where Orange's Collaborate was about external systems and Green's was about relational fields, Yellow's is about the *interior architecture of consciousness itself*.

You're learning to work with your own mind the way a musician works with an instrument. With precision. With care. With the understanding that mastery takes time, and that every moment of practice matters.
