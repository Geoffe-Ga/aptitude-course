## Teaching as Practice: Becoming a Holder of Space

At Clear Light, teaching is no longer something you "do"—it's an expression of who you are.

You don't need to start a meditation center or write a book (though you might). Teaching happens in every interaction.

**How the Adept Teaches:**

**1. Through Presence**

The most powerful teaching is your presence. When you're with someone, fully present, they feel it. They remember what it's like to be here. That's transmission.

**2. Through Reflection**

You reflect back what you hear. Not interpreting, not fixing, just mirroring. "It sounds like you're feeling stuck between two choices and both feel impossible." That simple reflection can be profoundly clarifying.

**3. Through Questions**

You don't give answers. You ask questions that help people find their own answers. "What would it look like if you trusted yourself here?" "What's the smallest next step?" "What are you actually afraid of?"

**4. Through Modeling**

You show them what it looks like to navigate difficulty without collapsing. To celebrate success without grasping. To be fully human and fully awake.

**5. Through Practice Invitations**

When someone's ready, you invite them to practice. Not evangelizing. Just offering. "Would you like to sit with me for 20 minutes?" "Have you ever tried journaling about this?" "What if you approached this with beginner's mind?"

**6. Through Holding Space**

Sometimes the most powerful thing you can do is just hold space. Sit with someone in their pain without trying to fix it. Let them feel what they're feeling. Be the sky that holds their storm.

This is how Adepts teach. Not through authority. Through availability.
