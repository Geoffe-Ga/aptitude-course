## The Bodhisattva Vow: Clear Light as Service

Clear Light is where practice stops being about you and becomes about everyone.

The Bodhisattva Vow, in Mahayana Buddhism, is the commitment to postpone one's own final enlightenment until all beings are liberated. It sounds impossible. Grandiose. How can one person liberate all beings?

But it's not literal. It's a statement of orientation: *I'm not doing this for me anymore. I'm doing this in service to the Whole.*

At Clear Light, you've touched the truth: there is no separation. Your liberation and others' liberation are not two things. When you wake up, the whole field wakes up a little. When you stabilize in presence, you create a resonance that others can attune to.

This is not spiritual ego. It's physics. You're a node in the network. When you change, the network changes.

The Bodhisattva Vow is your declaration: *I will use my practice, my presence, my capacity not to escape the world, but to serve it.*

This changes everything. Meditation is no longer self-care. It's *world-care*. You're sitting not just for your own peace, but for the peace of everyone you'll encounter today. You're cultivating presence so you can hold space for others' transformation.

This is the ethic of the Adept. And it's what makes the 90-minute sit sustainable. Because you're not grinding through it for personal benefit. You're offering it. And offerings don't deplete—they nourish.
