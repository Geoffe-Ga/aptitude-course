## You Cannot Stay on the Summit

Another teaching from *After the Ecstasy, the Laundry*:

**"You cannot stay on the summit forever; you have to come down again. So why bother in the first place? Just this: What is above knows what is below, but what is below does not know what is above. One climbs, one sees. One descends, one sees no longer, but one has seen."**

This is from René Daumal, and it captures the Wavelength perfectly.

You will touch the summit. You will have peak experiences. You will glimpse Clear Light. And then you will descend. Back into the valley. Back into the struggle. Back into the ordinary.

And that's not failure. That's the cycle.

The point of climbing is not to stay at the top. The point is to *see* from the top. And once you've seen, you carry that vision with you—even when you're back in the valley.

This is why the Adept doesn't chase peak states. They know that the summit is temporary. They know that the descent is inevitable. But they also know that the descent is not a loss. It's part of the rhythm.

As far as we ascend, so far can we fall. And that's okay. Because the falling teaches as much as the rising. The valley holds as much wisdom as the peak.

Clear Light is where you stop trying to stay on the summit. You climb. You see. You descend. And you trust that the seeing has changed you—even when you can't feel it anymore.
