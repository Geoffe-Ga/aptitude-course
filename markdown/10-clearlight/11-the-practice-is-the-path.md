## The Practice IS the Path

One final teaching to close this out:

**The practice IS the path.**

Not a means to an end. Not a technique to get you somewhere. The practice itself—showing up, sitting, breathing, witnessing—is the point.

There is no destination. There is no "arrival." There is just this moment. This breath. This sit.

And when you recognize that—when you stop practicing to *get* somewhere and start practicing because the practice is what's real—everything shifts.

You're no longer postponing your life until you're "enlightened." You're living now. Fully. Messily. Beautifully.

You're the Adept. Not because you've mastered anything. But because you've stopped pretending there's anything to master.

You've recognized the truth: you were always whole. The path was never about becoming whole—it was about *remembering* that you already are.

And now, you live from that remembering. Not perfectly. Not always. But more and more, as the practice deepens and the recognition stabilizes.

You sit. You walk. You work. You love. You fail. You begin again.

That's the practice. That's the path.

That's Clear Light.

And it's always been here. Waiting for you to notice.

Welcome home, Adept. The journey continues. Forever and always, just this.
