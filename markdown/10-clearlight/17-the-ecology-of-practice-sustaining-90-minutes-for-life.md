## The Ecology of Practice: Sustaining 90 Minutes for Life

Let's be practical. How do you actually maintain 90-minute sits, 4x/week, for the rest of your life?

**1. Design Your Environment**

Create a dedicated practice space. It doesn't have to be fancy. A corner of a room. A cushion. Maybe a candle. The consistency of place matters. Your nervous system learns: "When I sit here, this is what we do."

**2. Protect the Time**

Treat your practice time as non-negotiable. Not in a rigid way—in a sacred way. This is your appointment with the deepest truth of what you are. Everything else can wait 90 minutes.

**3. Build a Practice Community**

Solo practice is powerful. But practicing in community—even asynchronously—sustains you. Join a sangha (digital or local). Know that when you sit, others are sitting. You're part of a larger field.

**4. Track Without Attachment**

Keep a practice log. Note the date, duration, quality. But don't judge yourself by the data. You're tracking to see patterns, not to perform.

**5. Allow Flexibility in Form**

Some days you sit in perfect stillness. Some days you practice walking meditation or yoga or stream-of-consciousness writing. The form can flex. The commitment doesn't.

**6. Integrate Retreats**

At least once a year, do an extended retreat. 3 days. 7 days. 10 days if you can. Intensive practice accelerates integration in ways daily sits can't. It's like compound interest for your consciousness.

**7. Teach What You're Learning**

The best way to deepen your own practice is to share it. Write about it. Talk about it. Hold space for others. When you teach, you learn twice.
