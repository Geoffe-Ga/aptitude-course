## Green's Shadow: Spiritual Bypassing and Toxic Positivity

Green's shadow is subtle. It masquerades as healing. As compassion. As consciousness. But underneath, it's just another form of avoidance.

**Spiritual bypassing** is what happens when you use spiritual concepts to avoid dealing with your actual life. You forgive too quickly because "everything happens for a reason." You suppress your anger because "it's not spiritual." You stay in harmful situations because "it's teaching me a lesson." You mistake passivity for acceptance and call it enlightenment.

This is Green's trap. It's the person who's done so much inner work that they've convinced themselves they don't need to take action. Who can name their trauma but won't set a boundary. Who can articulate their needs but won't risk asking for them to be met.

Spiritual bypassing is the shadow side of shadow work. It's using the language of healing to stay stuck.

**Toxic positivity** is related but slightly different. It's the insistence that you must always be "high vibe." That negative emotions are signs of "low consciousness." That if you're struggling, it's because you're "not doing the work." It's the wellness culture that shames you for being human.

You see this in the person who responds to your grief with "But what's the lesson?" or your anger with "You're just creating more negativity." Who can't sit with discomfort—theirs or yours—so they try to positivity-bypass it away.

Both of these are Green at its worst. They're the refusal to actually feel the hard stuff. The insistence on skipping to "healed" without doing the messy work of healing.

The antidote is *discernment*. Ask yourself:
- Am I using this spiritual concept to avoid taking responsibility?
- Am I pretending to be okay when I'm actually not?
- Am I forcing positivity to avoid feeling pain?

Green's gift is embodiment. But embodiment means feeling *all of it*—not just the parts that are comfortable. It means meeting your rage, your grief, your shame without trying to transcend them too quickly.

Shadow work is not about getting rid of the shadow. It's about *integrating* it. And you can't integrate what you're still avoiding.
