## Ereshkigal: The Sister in the Shadow

Here's what most people miss about the myth: Ereshkigal is not evil. She's *grieving*. She's been exiled to the underworld, forgotten by the gods, left to rule over death and decay while her sister gets all the glory.

When Inanna descends, Ereshkigal kills her—not out of malice, but out of *rage* at being abandoned. She's the disowned part of the self, screaming for attention.

And here's the twist: the only thing that saves Inanna is when the emissaries witness Ereshkigal's pain without trying to fix it. They say, "Oh, your heart is in anguish. Oh, your suffering is great." They mirror her grief. And in being *seen*, Ereshkigal softens. She releases Inanna.

This is the secret of shadow work. Your shadow doesn't want to be defeated. It wants to be *witnessed*. It wants you to say, "I see you. I understand why you're here. I'm sorry I left you in the dark for so long."

When you meet your shadow with compassion instead of judgment, it stops fighting you. It becomes an ally.

That's the myth. That's the path. That's Green.
