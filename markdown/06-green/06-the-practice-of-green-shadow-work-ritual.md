## The Practice of Green: Shadow Work Ritual

We're at 30 minutes now—double Red, triple Purple. You're building the capacity for sustained, focused inner work. And the practice that does this most powerfully at Green is the **Shadow Work Ritual**.

This is not passive meditation. This is active alchemy. You're going into the basement of your psyche, turning on the lights, and inviting the monsters to tea.

There are many approaches to shadow work. The one we'll use here is a synthesis of Jungian active imagination, Gestalt chair work, and somatic processing. It's a 30-minute structured ritual that you'll do at least 4 times per week.

**The Structure:**

**1. Preparation (5 min)**

Set up two chairs facing each other. One is for your conscious self. The other is for your shadow.

Light a candle if that feels right. Put on instrumental music if you need ambient sound. Turn off your phone. This is sacred space.

Sit in the "self" chair. Close your eyes. Take a few deep breaths. Ground into your body. Place a hand on your heart and say aloud: "I am here to meet what I've been avoiding. I am ready to see."

**2. Invitation (5 min)**

Still in the self chair, call forward a specific shadow element. Pick one from your Shadow List. Maybe it's your rage. Your shame. Your people-pleasing. Your arrogance. Whatever feels most alive today.

Speak to it: "I know you're here. I know I've been ignoring you. I'm ready to listen now. Please show yourself."

Then sit quietly and let an image, a sensation, a voice emerge. Don't force it. Just wait. The shadow will respond—maybe as a younger version of you, maybe as a creature, maybe as a color or temperature in your body. Let it take whatever form it wants.

**3. The Switch—Becoming the Shadow (10 min)**

When you have a sense of the shadow's presence, physically move to the other chair. You are now *embodying* the shadow.

Sit in its posture. Feel its energy. Let it speak through you. Ask it:
- Why are you here?
- What do you need?
- What are you protecting me from?
- What gift do you carry?

Speak aloud in the shadow's voice. Let it rant, weep, rage, confess—whatever wants to come. Don't censor. This is the part of you that never gets airtime. Let it have the floor.

**4. The Return—Integration (5 min)**

Move back to the self chair. Take a breath. You're you again—but you've just heard from a part of you that's been exiled.

Now, speak to the shadow with compassion. Thank it for showing up. Acknowledge what it shared. Ask: "How can I honor you without letting you run my life? What would it look like for us to work together?"

Listen. Let an answer come. It might be simple: "Stop abandoning me when things get hard." "Let me have a voice." "Trust that I'm here to protect you, not destroy you."

**5. Closing—Sealing the Work (5 min)**

Place both hands on your heart. Speak a vow aloud: "I commit to integrating this part of myself. I will no longer exile you. We are one."

Blow out the candle (if you lit one). Journal for a few minutes about what came through. Don't analyze—just document. The insights will come later.

**Why It Works:**

The chair work externalizes the internal conflict. It gives the shadow a voice. And when the shadow is *heard*, it stops needing to sabotage you from the dark. It becomes a collaborator instead of a hijacker.

This is Carl Jung's method: you don't eliminate the shadow. You *befriend* it. You integrate it. You recognize that it's not your enemy—it's a part of you that got left behind, and it's been trying to get your attention ever since.

Every time you do this ritual, you reclaim a piece of your Wholeness. And over time, the parts that used to run you from the basement become allies in your conscious life.

That's the gift of Green. That's the practice.
