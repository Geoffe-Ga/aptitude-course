## Alternatives for Green Practice

Shadow work isn't one-size-fits-all. Some people can't sit in a chair and dialogue with their inner parts—it feels too weird, too dissociative, too triggering. That's okay. Here are other ways to do 30 minutes of embodied integration work.

**1. "Coal to Gold" Shadow Journaling**

Set a timer for 30 minutes. Pick one shadow element. Write about it in three phases:

- **Coal:** Describe the shadow element without judgment. What does it do? When does it show up? How does it feel in your body?
- **Pressure:** Explore the origin. When did you learn this pattern? What was the context? What need was it trying to meet?
- **Gold:** Reframe it. What's the gift hiding in this shadow? What wisdom does it carry? How can you honor this energy in a healthier way?

This is alchemical writing. You're transmuting the base metal of shame into the gold of self-knowledge.

**2. Gestalt Chair Work (Self vs. Shadow)**

Similar to the main practice, but simplified. Two chairs. Alternate every 5 minutes. Self speaks, then Shadow responds. Go back and forth for the full 30 minutes. Let the conversation unfold. By the end, you'll have clarity.

**3. Wording Through It (Paul Selig Method)**

Sit quietly. Place your hands on your heart. Ask aloud: "What would you have me know about [shadow element]?" Then write whatever comes, without filtering. Keep your hand moving for 30 minutes. This is channeling your own higher wisdom—letting the part of you that *knows* speak to the part that's confused.

**4. Tibetan Buddhist Emotion Transmutation**

Sit in meditation. Call forward a difficult emotion (anger, fear, shame, whatever). Don't try to change it or push it away. Just *be* with it. Notice where it lives in the body. Notice its texture, temperature, movement. Stay with it for 30 minutes. Watch it transform on its own. This is the practice of letting emotions alchemize through presence alone.

**5. Meditating on the Pain Body (Eckhart Tolle Method)**

Sit quietly. Scan your body for the place where old pain lives—what Tolle calls the "pain body." It's the knot of unprocessed emotion that resurfaces again and again. Place your attention there. Don't analyze. Don't try to fix. Just witness. Stay for 30 minutes. This is shadow work as pure awareness.

**6. Stream-of-Consciousness Letter to Your Most Repressed Self**

Write a letter to the part of you you've most rejected. "Dear Angry Me," "Dear Needy Me," "Dear Ashamed Me." Write for 30 minutes. Tell this part what you've learned. Apologize for abandoning it. Invite it home.

All of these work. Pick the one that calls to you. Experiment. Trust your intuition. The shadow will show up however it needs to.
