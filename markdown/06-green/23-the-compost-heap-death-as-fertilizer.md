## The Compost Heap: Death as Fertilizer

In permaculture, there's a principle: waste is food. What dies becomes the soil for what grows next. The rotting apple feeds the tree.

Shadow work operates on the same logic. The parts of you that you think are "bad"—the shame, the rage, the fear—they're not waste. They're *compost*. When you stop trying to eliminate them and start composting them (metabolizing them, integrating them), they become the richest soil for new growth.

Your anger? That's fertilizer for your boundaries.

Your shame? That's fertilizer for your self-compassion.

Your fear? That's fertilizer for your courage.

But you have to let them *die* first. You have to stop clinging to them as identity ("I'm an angry person") and let them break down into their constituent parts. Then they transform.

This is the alchemy of Green. This is the descent as practice. You go down into the rot, the decay, the death—and you discover that it's the source of all new life.
