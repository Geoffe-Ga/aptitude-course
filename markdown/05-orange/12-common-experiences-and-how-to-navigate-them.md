## Common Experiences and How to Navigate Them

**Tingling and Cramping**

Totally normal. It's the alkalosis affecting nerve conduction. If it's uncomfortable, slow down your breathing slightly or take a break. This isn't a race.

**Lightheadedness or Dizziness**

Also normal, especially in the first few sessions. Your brain is adjusting to the shifted CO2 levels. If it's intense, pause. Breathe normally. Return when you're ready.

**Emotional Release**

Some people cry, laugh, or feel waves of emotion during or after the practice. Breathwork can unlock stored tension in the body. This is not a problem—it's a release. Let it move through you. Don't analyze it. Just feel it and let it go.

**Temporary Loss of Motor Control (Tetany)**

Rare, but possible. If your hands or feet cramp and lock up, it's because of the pH shift. It's temporary (it will pass within minutes) and not dangerous, but it's why you should never do this practice in water or while standing.

**Euphoria or Altered States**

Many people report feeling "high" after the practice. That's the oxygen saturation, the endorphin release, and the shift in nervous system state. Enjoy it. But don't chase it. The goal is not the high—it's the resilience you're building.
