## How to Structure Your Exercise Practice

Orange loves systems, so here's a simple framework.

**The Four Pillars of Physical Capacity**

1. **Strength**: Lift heavy things. This builds muscle, bone density, and metabolic health. It also builds confidence. There's something primal about feeling strong.

2. **Cardio**: Get your heart rate up. This trains your cardiovascular system, improves endurance, and floods your brain with oxygen. Running, cycling, rowing, swimming—pick one.

3. **Mobility**: Stretch. Move through full ranges of motion. Yoga, dynamic stretching, or dedicated mobility work. This prevents injury and keeps your nervous system supple.

4. **Play**: Do something fun. Dance. Climb. Play a sport. This is where exercise stops being work and starts being joy. And joy is a form of resilience.

You don't have to do all four every session. But across the week, touch all four pillars.

**Example Weekly Structure:**

- Monday: Strength training (45 min)
- Tuesday: Cardio (30 min run or bike)
- Wednesday: Mobility + play (yoga or dance, 30 min)
- Thursday: Strength training (45 min)
- Friday: Rest or gentle movement
- Saturday: Long cardio or play (60 min hike, sport, etc.)
- Sunday: Mobility or rest

This is not prescriptive. Adapt it to your life. The key is *consistency over intensity*. Four moderate workouts per week beats one heroic workout followed by six days of nothing.
