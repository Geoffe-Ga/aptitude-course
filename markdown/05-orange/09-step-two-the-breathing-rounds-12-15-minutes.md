## Step Two: The Breathing Rounds (12-15 minutes)

You'll do three full rounds of controlled hyperventilation followed by breath retention. Each round deepens the effect.

**Round One: Awakening the System**

Take 30 to 40 deep, powerful breaths. Inhale fully through your nose or mouth—whatever feels natural. Fill your belly first, then your chest, then the upper lungs. You're inflating completely. Then exhale, but don't force it. Let the breath fall out like a sigh. The exhale is passive; the inhale is active.

The rhythm is continuous. Like waves rolling in and out. Breath after breath after breath. No pause between them. You're flooding your bloodstream with oxygen and purging carbon dioxide. Your blood pH will shift. Your nervous system will respond.

Within 20-30 breaths, you'll likely feel tingling in your hands, feet, or face. Your head might feel light. You might feel a sense of spaciousness, euphoria, or slight disorientation. This is normal. This is the chemistry changing. Keep breathing.

After the 30th or 40th breath, exhale fully and hold your breath with your lungs empty. Don't force the exhale—just let it go naturally, then stop.

Now: wait.

Your body will start to signal that it wants air. The diaphragm will contract. The throat will tighten. There will be a rising sense of urgency. This is the CO2 reflex kicking in—your body's alarm system saying, "We need to breathe!"

But here's the secret: you don't. Not yet. You've just oxygenated your blood beyond normal levels. You have time.

Stay calm. Relax your shoulders. Soften your jaw. Notice the sensations without reacting to them. This is where the training happens. This is where you learn that panic is optional.

When the urge to breathe becomes strong—not unbearable, just strong—take a deep, full recovery breath. Fill your lungs completely. Hold that breath for 15 seconds. Then exhale slowly and return to normal breathing.

Notice how you feel. Spacious? Calm? Energized? Write it down later.

Rest for 30-60 seconds. Then begin Round Two.

**Round Two: Deepening Trust**

Repeat the exact same process. 30-40 breaths. Full exhale. Retention. Recovery breath.

But notice: this time, you'll likely be able to hold your breath longer. Your body remembers. It's adapting. It's learning to trust that you know what you're doing.

This is not about "pushing through." It's about expanding your window of tolerance. Training your nervous system to stay regulated under conditions it used to perceive as emergency.

When you take your recovery breath this time, hold it for 15 seconds and really feel the fullness. The oxygen saturating every cell. The aliveness.

Then exhale and rest.

**Round Three: Mastery**

One more cycle. Same structure. But by now, the practice has a different quality. You're not fighting your body. You're dancing with it.

The breath retention on this round might surprise you. Two minutes. Three. Some practitioners go much longer. But that's not the point. The point is presence. The point is learning that you are not your survival reflexes—you are the awareness that can *witness* them and choose how to respond.

When you finish the third round, sit or lie quietly for a few minutes. Let the energy settle. Notice the quality of your mind. The clarity. The calm. The sense of capacity.

This is what Orange offers: the direct, embodied experience of expanding your own limits through systematic practice.
