## "ABSORB (Do/Feel)"—Full 6-Phase Wavelength Breakdown

Ultraviolet's Wavelength is the rhythm of deepening realization. Each phase is a movement in the dance of awakening—and each has its medicine and poison.

### 1. RISING

**Rx: Deepening | OD: Transcending**

Rising at Ultraviolet is about *going deeper*. Not trying something new—refining what you already know. You're deepening into subtler states, more refined concentration, clearer seeing.

**Deepening** is the natural refinement that comes from sustained practice. You sit every day. You go on retreat. And slowly, inevitably, the mind becomes more subtle. The jhanas become accessible. Insight becomes more frequent.

This is not forced. It's the fruit of consistency. You're deepening because the path naturally develops subtlety when you walk it faithfully.

But when the desire to transcend becomes compulsive, Rising tips into **Transcending**.

**Transcending** is the spiritual bypass. It's the urge to *escape* the human experience rather than *include* it. You're chasing the high states. Using meditation to avoid your life. Mistaking detachment for wisdom.

You see this in the person who's "too spiritual" to deal with their taxes, their relationships, their health. Who's always talking about enlightenment but can't hold down a job. Who's transcended so much they've forgotten how to be human.

The medicine is embodiment. Yes, ascend. But *bring the body with you*. Don't leave the earth to reach the sky. Integrate, don't bypass.

### 2. PEAKING

**Rx: Clarity | OD: Superiority**

Peaking at Ultraviolet is when everything becomes *clear*. The teachings land. The jhanas stabilize. Insight arises spontaneously. You're in the flow of the path.

**Clarity** is seeing reality as it is. Impermanent. Unsatisfactory. Empty of inherent self. Not as concepts, but as *lived truth*. This is what the entire path has been pointing toward.

Clarity is liberating. It cuts through confusion. It dissolves doubt. It reveals the structure of suffering and the way out.

But when Clarity becomes identification, Peaking tips into **Superiority**.

**Superiority** is the ego saying, "I've figured it out. I'm awake. I'm beyond." It's using your realization as proof that you're better than others. It's the most toxic shadow Ultraviolet has.

You see this in the teacher who can't be questioned. The practitioner who looks down on anyone expressing a different stage. The person who's weaponized their clarity.

The antidote is remembering: the one who sees clearly is empty. There's no "you" who's superior. There's just seeing. And that seeing is available to everyone—it's not your personal achievement.

Clarity is medicine. Superiority is poison. Stay humble.

### 3. WITHDRAWAL

**Rx: Discernment | OD: Judgment**

Withdrawal at Ultraviolet is the capacity to *discern* without *judging*. To see differences without making some "better" and some "worse."

**Discernment** is precision. You can tell when someone's operating from Red. You can see when a teaching is partial. You can recognize when you're being sold spiritual materialism. And you can name it clearly.

Discernment is necessary. It's what allows you to navigate complexity without getting lost. It's the Masculine's gift of seeing distinctions.

But when Discernment hardens, Withdrawal tips into **Judgment**.

**Judgment** is using your clarity to condemn. To dismiss. To reject. You see someone expressing Red or Orange and you think they're "wrong." You hear a teaching that doesn't resonate and you label it "inferior."

This is the shadow of hierarchy. Instead of honoring the spiral, you're using it to elevate yourself and diminish others.

The medicine is compassion. Yes, discern. But do it with love. Recognize that everyone is doing the best they can with the tools they have. That you were once at every stage you now see others inhabiting. That judgment is just fear dressed up as wisdom.

Discernment is medicine. Judgment is poison. Choose compassion.

### 4. DIMINISHING

**Rx: Humility | OD: Humiliation**

Diminishing at Ultraviolet is where the path humbles you. Where you see how much you *don't* know. How vast the mystery is. How small your understanding.

**Humility** is the recognition that you are not the master. You are the student. Always. No matter how far you've come, there's further to go. No matter how clear you've become, there are depths you haven't touched.

Humility is bowing to the dharma. To the lineage. To the teachers who came before. It's the recognition that you didn't invent this path—you're just walking it.

This is medicine. It keeps you open. Curious. Teachable.

But when Humility tips into self-flagellation, Diminishing becomes **Humiliation**.

**Humiliation** is the belief that you're *not enough*. That you'll never "get there." That everyone else is more awake, more clear, more devoted. It's the impostor syndrome of the spiritual path.

This is the shadow of Diminishing. Instead of healthy humility, you're collapsing into shame. You're using the vastness of the path as evidence of your inadequacy.

The antidote is trust. Yes, you have more to learn. But you're *on the path*. You're doing the work. And that's enough. You don't need to be enlightened to be worthy. You're worthy because you *are*.

Humility is medicine. Humiliation is poison. Choose trust.

### 5. BOTTOMING OUT

**Rx: Devotion | OD: Dogma**

Bottoming Out at Ultraviolet is where practice becomes *devotion*. You're no longer practicing to get somewhere. You're practicing because the practice itself is sacred. Because it's what you do. Because it's *who you are*.

**Devotion** is showing up every day. Sitting retreats even when they're hard. Bowing to the teachings. Honoring the lineage. It's the recognition that the path is not just a technique—it's a *transmission*. And you're part of it.

Devotion is what carries you through the dry periods. The dark nights. The moments when nothing makes sense. You keep practicing because your commitment is deeper than your moods.

This is the heart of Ultraviolet. This is what makes awakening possible.

But when Devotion becomes rigid, Bottoming Out tips into **Dogma**.

**Dogma** is mistaking the map for the territory. It's believing that *your* tradition is the only true one. That *your* teacher has the only real transmission. That anyone who practices differently is doing it wrong.

This is the shadow. You've become so devoted to the form that you've forgotten the essence. You're defending the teachings instead of *living* them.

The medicine is flexibility. Yes, honor your tradition. But hold it lightly. Recognize that there are many paths up the mountain. That the dharma is vast. That your way is not the only way.

Devotion is medicine. Dogma is poison. Stay open.

### 6. RESTORATION

**Rx: Recommitment | OD: Righteousness**

Restoration at Ultraviolet is where you renew your vow. You've been through the cycle. You've seen the highs and the lows. And now you choose, again, to keep going.

**Recommitment** is the practice of returning. Not because you have to, but because you *want to*. Because the path is alive in you. Because there's nowhere else you'd rather be.

This is not grim. This is joyful. You're not resigning yourself to a life of practice—you're *choosing* it, again, with full knowledge of what it costs and what it offers.

Recommitment is freedom. You're not bound by obligation. You're bound by love.

But when Recommitment becomes performative, Restoration tips into **Righteousness**.

**Righteousness** is using your devotion to judge others. "I sit every day. I go on retreat. I'm *serious* about this." And implicit in that is: "You're not."

This is the shadow. You've turned practice into a badge. A way to feel superior. A way to prove you're "doing it right."

The antidote is playfulness. Yes, commit. But don't make it heavy. Don't turn it into another form of achieving. Practice because it's beautiful. Because it's what you love. Not because it makes you better than anyone.

Recommitment is medicine. Righteousness is poison. Stay light.
