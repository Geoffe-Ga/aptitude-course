## The Dark Night: Ultraviolet's Inevitable Passage

At some point in Ultraviolet, you will hit what St. John of the Cross called "the dark night of the soul." This is not metaphor. This is a predictable stage of advanced practice.

Here's what happens: You've had profound experiences. You've touched states of unity, bliss, clarity. You know awakening is real because you've tasted it.

And then it's gone. And it doesn't come back. No matter how much you meditate. No matter how perfectly you practice. You sit for 75 minutes and it's just... dry. Empty. Nothing.

The old strategies don't work. The practices that used to produce states produce nothing. You feel like you've regressed. Like you've lost everything you gained.

This is the Dark Night. And it's not a problem—it's a *transition*.

**What's Actually Happening**

You're being asked to let go of *spiritual experience* as the goal. To stop chasing states. To release your attachment to even the highest forms of attainment.

The Dark Night strips you of everything you were clinging to—including your spiritual identity. It asks: Can you practice when practice gives you nothing? Can you sit when sitting feels meaningless?

If you can—if you keep showing up even when it's dry, even when you don't "feel" anything—you pass through to the other side.

And on the other side: equanimity so deep it doesn't depend on states. Presence so stable it doesn't require special experiences. You're free. Not because you've achieved something, but because you've finally stopped trying.

**How to Navigate the Dark Night**

1. **Don't quit.** This is the most important thing. Keep practicing. Even when—especially when—it feels pointless.

2. **Don't chase the return of states.** They're gone for a reason. Let them be gone. Grieve if you need to. But don't try to recreate them.

3. **Get support.** Talk to a teacher, a therapist, someone who's been through this. The Dark Night is isolating. Don't do it alone.

4. **Trust the process.** This is not regression. This is evolution. You're being asked to deepen in a way you can't yet see.

5. **Serve anyway.** Even when your practice feels dead, you can still show up for others. Sometimes that's what keeps you going.

The Dark Night ends. It always ends. And what emerges is someone who no longer needs *anything* from practice. Who sits because sitting is what they do. Who's free.
