## The Void Practice: Advanced Meditation Techniques for Ultraviolet

At 75 minutes, you're ready for more advanced methods. Here's one that's particularly suited to Ultraviolet: **Void Practice**.

This is a synthesis of Tibetan *tögal* (leapfrogging), Zen's "just sitting," and Advaita's *neti neti* (not this, not this). It's designed to destabilize the sense of separate self and rest in pure awareness.

**The Structure (75 minutes):**

**Minutes 1-15: Settling into Formlessness**

Sit. Eyes open or closed (try open—it trains you to witness even with sensory input). Don't focus on anything. Just be aware. When attention lands on a thought, a sound, a sensation, silently note: "*Not this*."

Not this thought. Not this sound. Not this sensation. You're not rejecting them. You're just recognizing: awareness is not *this*. It's what notices *this*.

**Minutes 15-30: The Inquiry**

Ask: "*Who is aware?*" Don't answer conceptually. Just let the question hang. Feel into it. Who is aware of the breath? Who is aware of the thoughts? Who is aware of the one asking?

You might feel a kind of vertigo. A sense of groundlessness. That's good. You're approaching the edge of the constructed self. Stay with it.

**Minutes 30-50: Resting as No-Thing**

You've been dismantling. Now rest. Don't try to be present. Don't try to witness. Just... stop. Let awareness be aware of itself. No object. No subject. Just the field.

If thoughts arise, fine. If sensations arise, fine. You're not focused on them. You're resting in the space that holds them.

This might feel like nothing is happening. Good. Nothing *is* happening. You're not doing anything. You're just being awareness being aware.

**Minutes 50-65: The Return**

Slowly, awareness begins to re-engage with form. You notice the breath again. The body. The sounds. But you're not *in* the body. You're the awareness *of* the body. The distinction is subtle but crucial.

You're returning to the world of form, but you're not forgetting formlessness. You're holding both.

**Minutes 65-75: Integration and Offering**

For the final segment, bring to mind all beings. Everyone who's suffering. Everyone who's seeking. Everyone who's lost.

And offer the merit of your practice: "May whatever clarity I've touched here serve the Whole. May it contribute to the liberation of all beings."

This is not performative. This is the Bodhisattva ethic. You're recognizing: your practice is never just for you.

**Why This Works**

Void Practice systematically deconstructs the sense of separate self. By refusing to identify with any arising phenomenon ("*not this*"), you train awareness to rest in itself.

Over time, the separate self is revealed as a construct—useful, functional, but not ultimately real. And in seeing that, you're free.

Not free *from* the self (you still need it to function). Free *within* it. You know it's a role you're playing, not what you actually are.

This is the realization Ultraviolet cultivates.
