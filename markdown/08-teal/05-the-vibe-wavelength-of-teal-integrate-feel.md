## The Mode of the Wavelength of Teal: Integrate (Feel)

Teal continues the **Integrate** Mode but shifts from **Do** (Yellow) to **Feel**. Where Yellow integrated through analysis and discipline, Teal integrates through *receptivity*. You're not building synthesis—you're *allowing* it to emerge.

This is the Wavelength of the mystic. The artist in flow. The shaman in ceremony. You're working with forces larger than your conscious mind. You're trusting the intelligence of the body, the unconscious, the field.

The six phases of Teal's Wavelength are identical to Yellow's in structure (because they're both Integrate Mode) but the *quality* is different. Where Yellow's Wavelength was focused and directed, Teal's is soft and receptive.

The phases are the same names we explored in Yellow:

**Rising: Rx = Focus | OD = Hyperfocus**

**Peaking: Rx = Concentration | OD = Obsession**

**Withdrawal: Rx = Adjustment | OD = Stagnation**

**Diminishing: Rx = Reorienting | OD = Dithering**

**Bottoming Out: Rx = Settling | OD = Depression**

**Restoration: Rx = Reset | OD = Addiction**

But the *experience* of each phase is different. At Yellow, you were actively managing the Wavelength—noticing drift and correcting. At Teal, you're *flowing with* the Wavelength—trusting that the cycle will move on its own.

We'll explore the felt sense of each phase in the breakdown. But first, note this: Teal's practice is less about *controlling* attention and more about *surrendering* to awareness. You're not trying to make anything happen. You're just being present with what is.
