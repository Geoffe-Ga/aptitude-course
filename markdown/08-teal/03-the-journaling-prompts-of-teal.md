## The Journaling Prompts of Teal

Teal's journaling is contemplative. You're not strategizing (Orange) or processing shadow (Green) or mapping patterns (Yellow). You're *reflecting*. Letting insights arise from stillness. Trusting the wisdom that emerges when you stop trying to figure everything out.

**Prompt 1: Reflect on Moments of Flow and Presence (At least 4x per week)**

Think back over your day or week. When did you experience genuine presence? When were you so absorbed in what you were doing that the sense of "I" temporarily dissolved?

Maybe it was during your practice. Maybe it was while cooking. Maybe it was in conversation, or walking in nature, or creating something.

Write about these moments. Not analytically—*descriptively*. What did it feel like? What were you doing? What conditions allowed that presence to arise?

Then ask: How can I create more of these conditions? Not in a grasping way—just in a curious, experimental way. What invites presence? What blocks it?

This is Teal's practice: learning to recognize the conditions that support awakening and then gently, playfully arranging your life around them.

**Prompt 2: Describe Experiences of True Self Guidance (At least 4x per week)**

When have you received guidance, insight, or knowing that seemed to come from beyond your thinking mind?

Maybe you asked a question and an answer arrived that surprised you. Maybe you felt a clear "yes" or "no" in your body that your intellect couldn't explain. Maybe you received an image, a phrase, a sudden clarity about what to do next—and it felt *true* at a soul level.

Write about these experiences. Document them. Not to prove anything, but to strengthen the connection. Each time you recognize True Self guidance, you're building trust. You're learning to distinguish between the voice of the personality (anxious, controlling, analyzing) and the voice of the True Self (calm, clear, knowing).

The more you honor these moments of guidance, the more available the True Self becomes. This is not forced. It's *invited*.

**Prompt 3: Journal About "Beginner's Mind" Experiences (At least 4x per week)**

Beginner's Mind is Suzuki's term for seeing reality without the filter of concepts, expectations, or past conditioning. It's meeting this moment as if you've never met any moment before.

When have you experienced this? When have you looked at something familiar—a tree, your partner's face, your own hand—and seen it *fresh*?

Write about it. Let yourself re-enter that quality of perception.

Then explore: What keeps me from seeing this way more often? What stories, judgments, and habits obscure the raw is-ness of reality?

Teal is about softening those filters. Not destroying them (you need concepts to function), but holding them lightly enough that you can occasionally set them down and just *see*.

**Prompt 4: Explore the Relationship Between Effort and Effortlessness (At least 4x per week)**

This is Teal's central koan: How do you practice without striving? How do you commit to the path without grasping for results?

Journal about your relationship to effort. When does it feel generative? When does it feel forced? When have you experienced the paradox of trying *and* letting go at the same time?

There's no right answer here. The point is to sit with the question. To let it work on you. To notice how even the question itself can become a practice.
