## The Default Habit of Teal: Baby Waterfall Conventions

Teal's habit is subtle but profound: **Baby Waterfall Conventions**.

A "Baby Waterfall" is Geoff's term for a micro-practice that brings you back to presence. It's a tiny intervention—so small it's almost invisible—that interrupts the autopilot and reminds you that you're alive.

Examples:
- Pausing for three breaths before checking your phone
- Feeling your feet on the ground before entering a room
- Placing your hand on your heart before responding to a text
- Noticing the sky once an hour
- Taking one conscious breath before eating

These are *conventions*—practices you build into your daily rhythm. They're not big. They're not dramatic. But they accumulate. Over weeks and months, these tiny pauses rewire your baseline state from "autopilot" to "aware."

The habit is to establish *at least three* Baby Waterfall Conventions and practice them daily. Pick moments in your routine where you're usually zoned out and turn them into doorways back to presence.

Why is this Teal's habit? Because Teal is about integrating the Witness into ordinary life. It's not about peak experiences. It's about waking up in the middle of the mundane. And Baby Waterfalls are how you do that.

Each pause is a reminder: You are not your thoughts. You are not your to-do list. You are the awareness that holds it all. And that awareness is always here. You just forget to notice.

Baby Waterfalls help you remember.
