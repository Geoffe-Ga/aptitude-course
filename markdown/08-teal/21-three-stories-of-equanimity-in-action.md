## Three Stories of Equanimity in Action

Let me show you what this looks like in real life.

**Story 1: The Diagnosis**

You get the call. The test results are back. It's cancer. Treatable, but serious.

The old you would spiral. Catastrophize. Collapse into fear or rage or denial.

The Teal you feels all of that—the fear is real, the grief is real—but you're also resting in the part of you that's *watching* the fear. You're the sky holding the storm.

You feel the sensations in your body. The tightness in your chest. The nausea. The racing thoughts. And you breathe. You return to presence. Again. And again.

You're not pretending it's fine. You're not bypassing the terror. You're *with* it. Fully. But you're not *only* it. You're also the awareness that holds it.

And from that space, you can think clearly. Make decisions. Ask for support. Do what needs to be done.

This is equanimity. Not invulnerability. Just the capacity to stay present in the storm.

**Story 2: The Betrayal**

Someone you trusted deeply lied to you. Publicly. The relationship is shattered.

The hurt is immense. The rage is justified. And you feel it all. You don't suppress it. You don't spiritually bypass it with "everything happens for a reason."

But you also don't *act* from it. You pause. You sit with the pain. You let it move through you like weather.

And in that pause, something shifts. You see: this person was operating from their own wound. Their betrayal says more about them than about you. You're not excusing it. But you're not making it mean you're unlovable.

You set a boundary. You end the relationship, if that's what's needed. But you do it cleanly. Without venom. Without the need to make them suffer.

This is equanimity. Not passivity. Not forgiveness-as-self-abandonment. Just the capacity to respond instead of react.

**Story 3: The Success**

You achieve the thing. The book deal. The promotion. The recognition you've been working toward for years.

And it feels... good. Really good. You let yourself feel it. The pride. The joy. The validation.

But you also notice: this doesn't *complete* you. It doesn't finally make you "enough." It's a moment. A good one. But it will pass, like everything passes.

So you celebrate. Fully. And then you let it go. You don't cling. You don't make it your identity. You don't need the next achievement to maintain the high.

You return to the practice. To the process. To the Witness that was here before the success and will be here after it fades.

This is equanimity. Not self-sabotage. Not the refusal to enjoy success. Just the wisdom of impermanence. The recognition that you are not your achievements. You are the awareness in which achievements arise.
