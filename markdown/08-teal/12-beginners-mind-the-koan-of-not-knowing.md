## Beginner's Mind: The Koan of Not-Knowing

Shunryu Suzuki's teaching is central to Teal: "In the beginner's mind there are many possibilities. In the expert's mind there are few."

This is not about pretending you don't know things. It's about meeting each moment as if it's the first time—because it is. This breath has never happened before. This conversation is new. This problem, even if it looks familiar, is arising in a new context.

Beginner's Mind is the antidote to Teal's shadow—the tendency to think you've "figured it out." To become attached to your realization. To turn awakening into identity.

The practice is simple: approach everything with curiosity instead of certainty. Even your most deeply held insights—hold them lightly. Be willing to be wrong. Be willing to be surprised.

This is how you stay open. How you keep growing. How you avoid calcifying into spiritual ego.


If walking isn't accessible—due to mobility issues, weather, or lack of safe outdoor space—here are practices that cultivate the same receptive, integrative awareness:

**1. Clairaudient Sitting Meditation (45 min)**

Sit in a comfortable position. Instead of focusing on the breath (Yellow), you're listening for the "still small voice"—the intuitive guidance that arises when the mind is quiet. Ask a question. Then sit in receptive silence. Let the answer come.

**2. Active Imagination (Jungian Method, 45 min)**

Sit quietly. Invite an inner figure to appear (a shadow part, a wise elder, an animal guide). Then *dialogue* with it. Ask questions. Let it respond. Write down the exchange or simply hold it in awareness. This is shamanism turned inward.

**3. Subtle Energy Scanning (45 min)**

Lie down or sit. Spend the full 45 minutes slowly, carefully scanning your body for subtle energetic sensations. You're not trying to change anything—just *witnessing* the flow. This is integration at the somatic level.

**4. Tarot Draw as Contemplation (45 min)**

Pull a card. Spend 45 minutes with it. Not reading a book about its meaning, but *gazing* at it. Letting it speak to you. Journaling what arises. Allowing the image to unlock something in your unconscious. This is divination as meditation.

**5. "Obstacles Are the Path" Reflection (45 min)**

Identify a current challenge. Sit with it for 45 minutes—not to solve it, but to *be with* it. Ask: What is this obstacle teaching me? How is it shaping me? What would it look like to embrace it instead of resisting it? This is Teal's core teaching embodied.

**6. Tara Brach's "Sacred Pause" Extended (45 min)**

Take Tara Brach's practice of the Sacred Pause—stopping, feeling, allowing, nurturing—and extend it to 45 minutes. You're creating a container of radical self-compassion and just *being* in it.

All of these work. But try the walk first. The body needs to move. The soul needs the earth.
