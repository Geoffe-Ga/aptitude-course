## Baby Waterfall Conventions: The Architecture of Micro-Practice

The Baby Waterfall Convention is Teal's signature contribution to habit design. It's deceptively simple: create tiny rituals throughout your day that serve as "reset points"—moments where you drop back into presence.

Here's why they work:

**1. They're Too Small to Resist**

A convention is 10-30 seconds. Three breaths. Feeling your feet. A moment of gratitude. Your resistance can't mobilize against something that brief. By the time your ego notices, it's already done.

**2. They Accumulate**

Do five conventions a day and that's 2-3 minutes of presence practice woven into ordinary life. Over a week: 15-20 minutes. Over a month: an hour. Over a year: 12+ hours of micro-meditation, embedded in your daily routine, requiring almost no willpower.

**3. They Anchor the Witness**

Each convention is a reminder: *Oh right. I'm here. I'm aware.* That recognition—even for 10 seconds—strengthens the Witness. Over time, these micro-moments compound into a baseline of presence.

**Examples of Baby Waterfall Conventions:**

- **Before eating**: Place hands on heart. Take three breaths. Feel gratitude for the food.

- **Before entering a meeting**: Feel your feet on the ground. Notice the sensations. Take one breath.

- **Before checking your phone**: Pause. Ask: "Am I choosing this, or is this compulsive?" Then proceed (or don't).

- **Before speaking in conflict**: Three breaths. Feel the sensations in your body. Then respond.

- **Before bed**: Scan your body from head to toe. Notice where you're holding tension. Release it.

The key is *consistency*. Pick 3-5 conventions. Do them daily. Let them become automatic. Over time, they become portals—tiny doorways back to presence, available anytime.
