## “INHABIT (Do)”

Approaching the Archetypal Wavelength from the perspective of Beige, one option is to observe and improve the energetic rhythm of building habits. In this Agentic Yes-And-Ness Aspect of Whole Adepthood, the Wavelength can be thought of as the core behaviors that take care of your meat.

These are the foundational behaviors that you repeat weekly, daily, hourly. Those that, when you zoom out and take stock of them in their entirety, bubble up into a personality that itself bubbles up into a destiny.

This Wavelength is Divine Masculine in flavor—not in material, embodied, or social gender, but in archetypal function:
active, directional, committed. It’s how you start to “Do” what must be done to stabilize your life, your energy, and your trust in yourself.

Where does “Inhabit” come from?

Because the Stage corresponds to personal action, I’ve chosen to depict the Beige Wavelength through the lens of what happens when you take accountability for your own motion through the ups and downs. You “Inhabit” it in the sense that this wave is lived from the inside—a personal experience mediated through your own will, agency, and authority.

The next Stage—the Purple, receptive “Yes” in Yes-And-Ness—will also be discussed through the lens of that Rising, Peaking, Withdrawal, Diminishing, Bottoming Out, and Restoration that you Inhabit within your personal experience. The difference will be that Purple, as a receptive Divine Feminine Stage, will be depicted as a “Feeling” wave.

This pattern will continue for all the hot (Divine Masculine, I, Individual, Self-Expressing) and cool (Divine Feminine, We, Collective, Self-Sacraficing) colors of the spiral of APTITUDE. For example the pair of Red’s Divine Masc “Doing” and Blue’s Divine Femme “Feeling” will look at the Wavelength from the perspective of what happens when you project your experience of the rhythms of life outside of yourself. Red looks at how to “Do” a healthy relationship with Power in relationship to others. Blue looks at how to “Feel” an effective, skillful experience of Relating to the outside world.

This is another part where I’m going to implore you not to worry about memorizing the information I am introducing. By the end of the next nine months, you’ll have a solid grasp on the motion of the Phases of the Wavelength and the shape of the Stages of the Spiral. I promise.

And as you’ll note, each point on this cycle contains both a Prescribed Dosage (Rx) and a Toxic Overdose (OD). Same energy, different outcome.

Last note: you can reference the image gallery if you ever want to refresh yourself on a specific Stage’s Wavelength expression. One way to use these graphics is as a helpful tool for reframing and harm reduction. When you begin to recognize that you’re tilting toward the Overdose expression of some Phase of the Wavelength, this course will introduce self-care strategies and Practices that can assist in venting the excess energy and allow you to course correct.


Across this program, we’re looking at fractal dimensions. At any zoom level, the Universe can be modeled as oscillating vibration. As Wavelengths composed of smaller Wavelengths.

Within the Spiral Stages of APTITUDE buzzing back and forth between hot and cold colors and then forever repeating again at the foundation, you’ll find the above image. A looping, spiraling ouroboros of effort and collapse. But zoom in even further and you will note yet another rhythmic pendulum swing. One between Overdose and Prescription.

The difference between medicine and poison is dosage.

~ Hippocrates (paraphrased)

This isn’t just ancient Greek wisdom from a toga’d apothecary. It’s felt truth.

It’s how I had been moving through life.

I’d briefly build habits, burn out, rebuild, and repeat. But that Sisyphean pattern—damned to heave a boulder up a hill and then have it flatten him, rolling back to the bottom just before he crests the hill’s top—is exactly what Energy Scaffolding is designed to heal.

Furthermore, you can course correct from Overdose to Prescription using this Stage’s Practice of Grounding, either with the 5-4-3-2-1 Technique or one of the alternatives I’ll present later on.

In my experience, simply recognizing that I am in an Overdose mode and noting, naming, or labeling that is often enough to reduce my activation level and gently let go of the extra dosage of energy.

Let’s take a look at the Beige Stage’s Wavelength through the lens of the poles of OD and Rx.

------------------------------------------------------------------------



### 1. RISING

- Rx: Commitment
- OD: Overcommitment

You undoubtedly recognize the mood state where energy blows into your system like nourishing oxygen after a too-long period underwater. That energy to finally be Doing something again after a period of overwhelm and inadequacy can be a godsend. The sense of confidence and joy… We’ll talk about the Feeling aspect of it later.


For now, just think about what it feels like to make a Commitment. A vow to yourself.
A promise to do better, be better. To grow. Often, I already feel my energy rising just through the plan to actualize aspiration.

But alas. I get puffed up on that feeling like a balloon stretched to bursting and before I know it I’ve signed up for six different self-improvement seminars, scheduled myself for daily 4 a.m. cold plunges, and promised to journal, juice, run and start a business by next week.

Yup. That’s Overcommitment. That’s the overdose. And it feels like rocket fuel—great until you get too high on it and end up crashing.

Energy Scaffolding Recommendation: Pick one habit. Commit like an ascetic householder monk. That’s what medicine looks like.

------------------------------------------------------------------------



### 2. PEAKING

- Rx: Diligence
- OD: Thriving

When the habit sticks, when the new practice feels good, when you're doing it every day and it’s working—good lord, does that high feel good.

Thriving sounds great. And it is! But we want to build up to it slowly, not swallow the whole world like a snake unhinging its jaw.
That voraciousness is seductive, but it's the Overdose form of momentum.


I’ve fallen prey to it so many times. I’ll feel unstoppable. I’ll be glowing, multitasking, flying through my day like a productivity god.

But here’s the truth: Unless it is built brick by brick over a realistic time horizon where each Habit can be fully integrated to the degree that maintaining its inertia is nearly effortless, Thriving is not sustainable. Diligence is.

Diligence is the subtle daily act of showing up without needing fireworks. I don’t need to feel like a god to know that I am winning. I’ve learned the joy of quiet effort. Of steady burn. It’s a piece of thriving, one that I can actually maintain.

Scaffolding Response: Don’t add more. Just stay the course. Resist the thrill of more.

------------------------------------------------------------------------



### 3. WITHDRAWAL

- Rx: Steadiness
- OD: Burnout


The Withdrawal of that energy of productivity is inevitable. All things move according to the rhythm of pendulum swings, and no Peak can be sustained forever.

This reduction in juice might happen imperceptibly at first. I’ll find myself a little more tired from one day to the next.
The Habit (or Habits) I’ve Committed (or Overcommitted) to feel a little harder.

Maybe I lapse once and gently forgive myself, just as I vowed I would… but I will have apparently not fully allowed myself the necessary grace because before long I’ve lapsed again. Lapsed and relapsed. Sometimes this feels like failing, but another option exists:
withdrawing.

Burnout happens to me most often when I get caught trying to unnaturally sustain a peak. Trying to keep my productive energy high enough to continue a Habit Stack when the insistence of my biorhythms is that it is time to contract. Time to turn inward.

Burning emergency fuel to keep my lanterns lit? That’s the overdose.

Steadiness, by contrast, is the skill of recognizing that I am in the beginning moments of the Withdrawal Phase and deliberately taking stock of what I am committed to, and what can be jettisoned.

Maybe I was doing a little extra while riding the Peak.
I might have been seduced by the temptation to Thrive even though I began with a simple, singular, “atomic” commitment. Instead of just building my cold shower habit, I decided to use my extra vim to devote myself to journaling and breathwork.

This is fine. Fairly common for surfers of the Wavelength like us. As long as I have the energy to overachieve, it isn’t going to hurt… but the second that the energy begins to Withdraw, it's time to refocus on my specific Commitment.
To gracefully, equanimously let go of everything else, no matter how much I enjoyed having those exciting extras in my life.

Scaffolding Response: Don’t panic. This is a normal part of the wave. It may be time to shift into Low Grit mode, or at minimum, to let go of anything that might be called an “Overcommitment.” Circle the wagons. Batten down the hatches. You can make it through the coming valleys. And the only way out, as you may recall, is through.

------------------------------------------------------------------------



### 4. DIMINISHING

- Rx: Security
- OD: Grasping


By the time that I’ve made it to the Diminishing phase of commitment to my habits, I am already distinctly feeling the overall ebb.

It’s common, at this point, for my brain to start rationalizing, questioning, and negotiating:

- “Is this even working?”
- “Maybe I should give up,”
- or just as often: “Can’t I go back to Thriving??”


I have become accustomed to recognizing these thought patterns as the orientation toward growth of Grasping. It’s the Overdose defined by trying to force outcomes. Grasping says: “If I just try harder, I can get back to the Peak.”

But it doesn’t work that way.

Again, just as it is often said about a bad trip on LSD or Magic Mushrooms: the only way out is through.

If I welcome contraction, it doesn’t have to represent a backsliding relapse into the energy that preceded my initial commitment.

### Come back and read this if you find yourself slipping and considering abandoning the project in its entirety.

I can only tell you what works for me, but I can avow sincerely that this attitude really works.

I no longer let the Sisyphean task of becoming Whole create a landslide just as I get to the top of the hill and my boulder starts slipping. Instead, I am now predisposed to intentionally let a few things go and make a plan to slowly add back the Habits that I had relapsed on, along with whatever new ones I have been itching to add (or subtract) since I initially engaged with Energy Scaffolding.

In my experience, every six to twelve months, a total re-invisioning of the Habit plan of Energy Scaffolding becomes necessary. New priorities have arisen. New obstacles have become unavoidable.

It happens to everyone, eventually. Given that we are incarnated as humans, we can’t help being beset by tragedy and inconvenience. A diagnosis, illness, serious injury, job loss, car breakdown, or death can set even the most advanced Adept back to the earliest stages of self-development and self-actualization.

That’s ok. The obstacles are part of the path.

When that happens, take stock of what you’ve been completing. Be fully, unflinchingly honest with yourself about what you have the capacity to work on right now. And then let everything else go. You can add habits back in three week increments once again.

Giving yourself that grace feels great. It’s what makes this practice sustainable. Gentle acceptance of the allowances we must make for life’s difficulties is what turns the obstacles themselves into part of the path.

And through practicing returning to commitment, again and again, viewing yourself as self-efficacious gets easier. This is how Free Will becomes not only possible, but an obvious explanation for why you do the things you do.

Even on the fractally smaller scale of the energy fluctuations of a single Habit adoption cycle, the medicine during the Diminishing Phase of the “Inhabit (Do)” Wavelength is Security—the rooted knowledge that your identity isn’t tied to performance.

After all, you are already Whole. You can’t achieve perfection any more than you can achieve having a pair of lungs. You incarnated here with both lungs and Wholeness. You’re already everything you aspire to be.

It’s okay to have down days. Try to remember that even in Low Grit mode, whether you are working hard to keep your promises to yourself or discerningly re-imagining what promises are possible, the Habits you have selected are still embedding themselves.

The rush of New Relationship Energy might fade, but what comes next is the stable marriage. The companionship between you and your True Self that endures, so Secure that you can hardly imagine its loss.

Scaffolding Response: Keep on working the program. Even a micro-dose counts. Let your habit be a stabilizer, rather than a savior.

------------------------------------------------------------------------



### 5. BOTTOMING OUT

- Rx: Planning
- OD: Overwhelm


The energy of Bottoming Out as represented by this Wavelength is extremely different for the Rx and the OD, but they are truly the same vibe at different dosages.

The inevitable valleys you will encounter in each creative act, habit building endeavor, or life optimization project don’t need to be a cause for fear and trepidation.

Although the Lows will come, I have found that when I resist resistance itself and lean in to Radical Acceptance — which we’ll talk more about during the next up Purple stage — I am able to shift from an attitude of Overwhelm to one of relaxed Planning—trusting tomorrow’s me with tomorrow’s problems.

Often the sense will come at 2 AM: you’ll be lying in an insomniac stupor running through all of the problems that demand your attention. When you find yourself worrying that your contributions are inadequate to prevent the coming AI, alien, racist, fascist, climate, political, public health, meaning, meta, poly, (etc) crises, it’s time to recognize that the apocalypse is not a job that needs an immediate solution from a sleep deprived Adept with a touch of the depressies.

The greatest gift you can give yourself—at least when it comes to Bottoming Out—is the permission to allow tomorrow’s problems to be taken on tomorrow. You don’t need to solve any crises when you’re struggling with burnout. You don’t even need to worry about your housework or the dayjob deadlines.

The pendulum will keep swinging. You’ll have energy again. You can tackle whatever it is that keeps you up at night later, once you’ve slept and energetically recharged.

I have made  “I don’t need to solve that right now” and “that’s not a problem for depressed Geoffe” my mantras for those late night sessions of self-flagellation.

Overwhelm is an OD of the feeling that you just don’t have the energy you need to dig yourself out of a future that might not come.

Planning is the alternative.

Planning—in its Bottoming Out dosage—isn’t doing. It’s the quiet act of deciding when and where a problem will get your attention, so that it can stop demanding it now. You get to rest in your low energy precisely because the next step has a place to live that isn’t your 2 AM mind. This is where all the work you’ve been putting in becomes deeply embedded. If you calibrate your sense of the necessity of rest toward the effective, you can vent off the extra Bottoming Out darkness that doesn’t serve you.

Scaffolding Response: Every emotion has a message. Every phase of the Wavelength serves a purpose. By listening when your body asks you to contract, you can honor its directives. You can avoid the collapse that comes from the inevitable explosion that arises when we try to pretend the shadow doesn’t exist.

### 6. RESTORATION

- Rx: Next Habit
- OD: New Plan


The relief of Restoration often feels like it can never come soon enough. We’ll discuss strategies to limit the duration of your low periods (and to maximize the productivity, inspiration, and good vibes of your high periods without getting the pendulum swinging too dangerously) later on in the course.

But when it comes to this “Inhabit” expression of the Archetypal Wavelength, where we are discussing this first Do-ing aspect of Wholeness, the Restoration phase takes on a slightly different meaning.

We aren’t talking about emotions like Recuperation, Catharsis, or Bitterness, although those are all characteristics of the Restoration Phase that we’ll explore in later Stages. We’re talking about the simple actions that comprise the work of keeping your promises to yourself, building habits and developing a sense of agency, self-efficacy, integrity and reliability.

So you’ve made it through the darkness. Now the energy is beginning to build again. You have to make a choice about what you’re going to do with it.

When it comes to the attitude of “Okay. What’s next?” how intensely you are relieved by the return of your productive energy can easily lend itself to the OD: a New Plan—glitz, glamor and flashy impatience, this is the sort of rebirth that’s actually just another overcommitment.

Here, and any time you recognize yourself behaving in a way that might qualify as one of the extremes characterized by the Overdose, watch the feeling, ground yourself, and allow the extra energy to dissipate on its own.
Reframe your thinking, let go of the reaching, grasping, aversion, or craving that is inspiring overexertion, and relax into the alternative:
the Medicine.

Here in Restoration, that looks like picking up the Next Habit in the scaffolding plan that you made for yourself. Utilize the surplus energy you’ve created. Don’t burn the fossil fuel of fantasy that can create a powerful explosion of productivity. It will wreck your inner ecosystem.

Scaffolding Response: Add the next habit from your spreadsheet. No rush. No overhaul. Avoid “shiny thing” syndrome at all costs. You made a promise to yourself. It’s time to be true to your word. When you learn to keep your commitments, something incredible happens. When you follow through on your intentions, you start to view yourself as, well, the kind of person who follows through on their intentions.

The kind of person with Free Will.

## In Summary

This expression of the Archetypal Wavelength shows us the trap of trying to “change everything at once” and the magic of letting momentum accrue through consistency. Energy Scaffolding isn't about burning bright and fast—it's about building energy slowly, habit by habit, the tortoise whose consistent, temperate strategy wins the race.

The Beige Stage of APTITUDE—the Divine Masculine root of the spiral—is about learning to “DO” with patience and integrity. If you keep your promises to yourself—did I mention that one of your habits should definitely be honesty?—self-efficacy is sure to follow.

It turns out the key to getting your shit together is not doing more, not overextending yourself with a total instant overhaul. It’s doing consciously: honoring the natural ebb and flow of energy that we all experience without being tipped over by it and wiping out.

> Think of discipline like a battery. Useful, but short-lived.

~ Julia Cameron, The Artist’s Way

Unless you can generate enthusiasm, this project will stall. The discipline to keep to the Rx expression of the Wavelength when you are in a challenging moment will be vital, but if you try to live exclusively from a place of “mastering” yourself, you will burn out. Develop a sense of play. Reward yourself for success. Remember to backslap yourself with praise for embodying your Aspirational Identities!

I am nobody special and this has worked for me. Stick to the process and it just might for you too!
