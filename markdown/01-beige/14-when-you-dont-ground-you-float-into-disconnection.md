## When You Don't Ground, You Float Into Disconnection

The Overconsumption Offramp leads to a high-frequency feedback loop. I have learned to recognize the feeling of being lifted too far from my body. Synchronicities abound at first, but then I get to looking for them too intensely. I end up with the psychotic obsessions with coincidence that the psychiatrists call delusions of reference.


I find myself spacing out mid-conversation or checking my phone while someone is talking for another cheap dopamine hit. I forget to eat. My car’s engine explodes. Or, god forbid, I start yet another new project to leapfrog the one that is still bleeding out.

This cannot last. The Peak can be prolonged, but under the weight of my own overexertion, eventually the inevitable contraction comes. But after the Offramp, the wave doesn’t slope anymore—it plummets.

“It seemed like a good idea at the time.” (Self-Destructive Shame, Anxiety, or Paranoia Inducing Explosion)

Hindsight is 20/20. In a prolonged Peak state, I’ve figured out a way to flood myself with dopamine. It feels fantastic, but as Steven Kotler says, “dopamine is not to be trusted.”

Convinced that I am invincible, I have made a lot of dumb, risky, or regrettable decisions.

And yeah, maybe some of them could have been good ideas—if they had come in a few months. But because I was trying to do too much, too fast, without integration, I stretch past the breaking point. When the branch I’ve climbed out onto snaps, it leads to shame, anxiety or paranoia.

The fall begins.

------------------------------------------------------------------------



“AHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH!” (Nosedive)

Have you felt this pure panic? The existential fear? My thoughts racing.
My mind preoccupied with wondering if I’ve blown it completely, forever.
Part of the reason the Nosedive sucks so bad is that I knew better. I saw contraction coming, but now my hubris has added kerosene to your fall.

Inevitably, I crash into The Pit of Despair:

“Is this thing bottomless?” (Pit of Despair)

The lowest low. Worse, much worse, than a normal Bottoming Out, because this one’s tinged with guilt, confusion, and the belief that I shouldn’t be here. Through my clinging, I have drawn the pendulum precariously high, and now I am dealing with its dauntingly low backswing.

The price of extended Highs are bitter Lows.

The balloon tied to your crown chakra has popped and dropped you back to Earth with no parachute.
