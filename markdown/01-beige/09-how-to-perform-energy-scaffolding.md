## How to Perform Energy Scaffolding

Step 1: Open a spreadsheet. Make four columns:
Habit, Energy Invested, Energy Returned, and Net Energy.

Step 2: Brainstorm like your hair is on fire. Write down every habit you’ve ever thought about starting (or stopping). No wrong answers. This is your dream buffet. You can have it all. I was overwhelmed when I wrote down everything that I wanted to quit—smoking, drinking, caffeine, doomscrolling—and everything I wanted to start—cold showers, vitamins, water, exercise, journaling, meditation, breathwork, Metta. But a year and a half later, every single one of those things is part of my life. Be honest. Be hopeful.
As
Be extra. You really can have it all—just not all at once.

Step 3: Energy Invested. Rate each habit from -10 to +10. A -10 means the habit would actually give you energy just to think about doing it. A +10 means it’s so hard to get yourself to do it that you might cry just imagining it. Trust your gut. You’re rating how much energy it takes to begin.

Step 4: Now hide that column. This next part works best when your brain isn’t trying to cheat.

Step 5: Energy Returned. Same scale, -10 to +10.
Except this time, rate how you feel afterward. A +10 means you’re floating, glowing, kissing babies. A -10 means the habit makes you feel like a husk. Again: honesty is sacred here.

Step 6: Unhide the first column and calculate Net Energy = Energy Returned - Energy Invested for each row. You’re now looking at a live blueprint of your energy ecosystem.

Step 7: Sort the spreadsheet. First by Net Energy (highest first), then by Energy Invested (lowest first), then Energy Returned (again, highest first). Boom. You’ve just found what behavior researcher Charles Duhig calls your “Keystone Habits”—the habits that give the most and cost the least. These are your low-hanging fruit and the perfect place to start.

Step 8: Create a new column: Start Date. Use the date you’re beginning the APTITUDE journey as the top row. Add 21 days to each row below. This is the pacing mechanism that makes everything work. Trust the rhythm.

Step 9: Add three more columns: Low Grit Goal, Clear Goal, Stretch Goal. Use the SMART method here:

- Specific: What exactly will
  you do? (e.g. Drink water)
- Measurable: How will you
  track it? (e.g. Ounces per day)
- Achievable: Is it realistic? “A” should also
  stand, however gently, for Ambitious.
- Relevant: Does it serve your
  Aspirational Identity? (More below.)
- Time-bound: What’s the time
  commitment? (This is kind of a given. You should be shooting for being fully integrated with each habit by the 21st morning).

Set three levels for each habit:

- Low Grit: So easy you’d be embarrassed not to do it.
  (Ex: “Make a special effort to drink 8oz of water, 3x per day.”)
- Clear Goal: Just right. Balanced and reasonable. (Ex: “Drink 8x8oz of water per day.”)
- Stretch Goal: The fantasy version. (Ex: “ONE. GALLON. DAILY.”)

Step 10: Aspirational Identity. Look at your top 10 habits. Ask: Who does this every day? What kind of archetype is slowly emerging here? Is it the Whole Adept? A Conscious Biohacker? A Loving Partner? A Pinterest Witch-Meets-Urban Monk hybrid? Create a name or symbol that resonates. It can even be a mythical or fictional character. Are you channeling the elephant god Ganesh, as biohacker and angel investor Tim Ferris does? Or maybe the dense data packet you’re choosing is more visual, like a pinterest vision board. Maybe the Aspirational Identity you wish to embody is Neo from the Matrix.

Choose something resonant. This is who you're becoming. Every time you show up for a habit, whisper to yourself: “That’s right. I AM \[insert badass aspirational self here\].”

#### Now, a few soft rules of magic and manifestation:

Honesty is non-negotiable. You’re not here to impress anyone. You’re here to learn to keep your promises to yourself. That’s what develops agency. It’s the -And in Yes-And-Ness. The moment you start noticing that you’re following through—because you can if you slow down and stay consistent—you’ll begin to believe in your own power again. A strong suggestion: you will benefit immensely from making one your chosen Habits a practice of Four Agreements-style “impeccable speech,” Buddhist “Right Speech,” the Yogic “Yama” of “Satya” which translates to truthfulness, or whatever other tradition’s definition of careful language you vibe with. By always telling the truth, especially to ourselves, we develop an immense, practical, magickal energy of self-respect and power.

Gentleness is power. You will break streaks. You will forget. You will mess up. And then you will return. That’s the muscle we’re building. Return. Return. Return. As flow researcher and peak performance journalist Steven Kotler says, “Impossible is just a checklist.” It’s okay to fall behind. Just don’t fall off the path.

Go slow. You’re going to want to do everything at once. That manic spark will whisper, “Change it all now.” But the secret is: deliberate pacing is what makes the change stick. In a moment, we’ll explore this more deeply via the Archetypal Wavelength of habit adoption—that Doing energy of the Inhabit mode.

Lastly, all grace is shared grace. There will be times you feel “selfish” for all this self-care and self-work. That couldn’t be farther from the truth. Granted, you will need to safeguard some time for some of these habits that will pull you away from your partner or your family. Your Energy Scaffolding plan might involve taking mindfulness or metta breaks at work. The people in your life may in fact object.

But the truth is that everything you’re working on here is rippling outward and affecting the cosmos, starting with the people closest to you.

A rising tide of consciousness raises all boats.

A Whole Adept who is more thoughtful, loving, caring, conscious, clear, present, awake, and luminous can’t help but raise the vibration of those they come in contact with. In the deep sense, we cannot work on ourselves without simultaneously affecting the groups and even the systems we are parts of.

In fact, self-actualization is a compulsory gateway to pass through for justice and systemic change! We’ll talk about getting into community and contributing to movements later, but don’t let anyone steer you away from this work by suggesting you are only being self-serving.

You may find that those same people end up wanting to join you for meditation once they see how much your self-work is improving your happiness, self-love, self-efficacy, and impact on the world.
