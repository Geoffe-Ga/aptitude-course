## And then… the dangerous temptation to repeat

Eventually, something cracks open again. You get a bit of your mojo back. You taste the memory of Thriving. And you say:

“That was terrible.
Think I’ll rubber-band rapidly back to Manic Overconsumption to cope.” (Re-Emergence)

It’s possible to go directly from the Pit of Despair back to the Overconsumption Offramp. Just look at my twenties as an example. I may not have taken a single breath that wasn’t drunk or hungover.

This can get really bad for some. At some point, we will all need to ground. I have tried the alternative. I’ve gone for the bottle to cure the withdrawal. I’ve picked up six new habits and sworn I’d cured myself. I’ve gone harder. Faster. Higher.

But of course, all I’ve accomplished is ensuring the Nosedive is once again inevitable.

Eventually we all need to ground. It will come for each of us. Whether it is through intentional practice and renunciation, or through prison or death. The ultimate grounding for someone too unmoored from their True Self’s Wavelength is to be buried six feet beneath the Earth.
