### 4. Philosophical Consistency Points

From Issue #1, the key reframing:

**OLD MODEL (Wilber/Graves):**
- Constant upward growth
- "Can't go backward"
- Later stages superior to earlier
- Integration AND transcendence (but transcendence emphasized)

**NEW MODEL (APTITUDE Chords):**
- Growth AND contraction both inevitable
- All stages remain available
- Each stage essential for wholeness
- Stages like notes on a scale - you play chords as needed
- Example: Negotiating a raise might need Blue (empathy) + Orange (ambition) + Beige (survival awareness)

**Metaphor Bank:**
- Musical: notes, chords, scales, songs, tuning, harmony
- Visual: palette, colors, full spectrum
- Embodied: capacities, expressions, modes

### 5. Special Considerations

**Don't lose nuance:**
- The 9-month course DOES move through stages sequentially for learning purposes
- Energy Scaffolding DOES build habits progressively
- Later practices ARE more complex (1min → 90min)
- This is about PEDAGOGY not HIERARCHY

**Framing to use:**
- "You learn the notes in sequence, but you play them all together"
- "The course teaches one stage at a time; life asks you to express them all"
- "Beige comes first in the curriculum not because it's inferior, but because it's foundational"

### 6. Test for Contradiction

Ask of each section:
1. Does this imply Stage 10 is "better than" Stage 1?
2. Does this suggest you leave earlier stages behind?
3. Would a reader think Beige is only for "beginners"?
4. Does this honor contraction as much as expansion?

If yes to any, revise.
