# Red Practice (Confidence)
A circular diagram illustrates how confidence follows the six phases of the Archetypal Wavelength. The cycle begins at **Rising**, labeled “1,” where a person practices confidence by placing a hand on the belly while breathing and focusing on the diaphragm. It moves to **Peaking**, “2,” showing awareness of belly movement and a confident, resonant voice. The next phase, **Withdrawal**, “3,” describes losing focus on the belly breath and experiencing insecurity or uncertainty. **Diminishing**, “4,” shows forgetting the breath entirely and speaking with a weak or nasal voice. **Bottoming Out**, “5,” depicts mind wandering or unconscious movements that compromise dignity or values. The cycle then turns upward into **Restoration**, “6,” where the person remembers the practice and refocuses. Two large red arrows loop around the diagram to show the downward and upward motion of the cycle. The title reads “Confidence is a Practice,” with a note stating it can be pursued formally or conjured in the moment.

# Blue Practice (Love)
The infographic presents the six-phase Archetypal Wavelength as a circular practice map for cultivating love, shown through blue descending and ascending arcs. At **Rising**, labeled “1,” the practitioner silently sends metta phrases, such as “I am Love through so and so; Word, I am Word.” At **Peaking**, “2,” love is felt as warm energy spreading from the chest, influencing perception, speech, and choices. **Withdrawal**, “3,” describes losing contact with the metta phrases and falling into dissatisfaction, judgment, or shame. **Diminishing**, “4,” shows forgetting the recipient of metta and reacting from an ungenerous or constricted place. **Bottoming Out**, “5,” represents completely forgetting the intention to practice metta and slipping into arguing, dominating, or other defensive behaviors. The cycle turns upward toward **Restoration**, “6,” where the practitioner remembers the practice and refocuses. Two curved blue arrows illustrate the downward and upward movement of the cycle. The title reads “LOVE is a Practice,” with a note that it can be pursued formally or invoked in the moment.

# Orange Practice (Attraction Self-Care)
The infographic depicts the “Attraction” half of the Archetypal Wavelength, shown as an upward-moving arc in shades of gold and orange. At the bottom of the arc is **Restoration**, where supportive, gentle activities such as taking a bath, getting sunshine, somatic meditation, biking, or dancing help rebuild energy. The arc rises into **Rising**, which lists practices that generate momentum—Wim Hof breathing, “Dog Walkin Shamanism,” deep conversation, yoga, and creative writing. At the top is **Peaking**, where high-energy practices like magick, micro-retreats, confidence work, kombucha brewing, or making music help sustain peak flow. A quote at the top reads, “When things are going well, don’t relax,” attributed to Chogyam Trungpa Rinpoche. A note explains that understanding which practices support you in each of these phases—from Restoration through Rising to Peaking—can enhance productivity, connection, creativity, achievement, and success. Two large gold arrows illustrate the upward trajectory of the Attraction cycle.

# Green Practice (Aversion Self-Care)
The infographic shows the “Aversion” half of the Archetypal Wavelength, depicted as a downward arc in pale and dark greens. A quote at the top reads, “When things are going poorly, don’t panic,” attributed to Chogyam Trungpa Rinpoche. Text beside the arc explains that low energy is inevitable and that everyone must traverse the full downswing, but intervening early makes the descent less painful.

At the top of the arc is **Withdrawal**, listing grounding or regulating practices such as the 5-4-3-2-1 sensory technique, intense exercise, 4-7-8 breathing, and box breathing. Below it is **Diminishing**, where long drives, hot beverages, walking, or journaling can help soften the slide. At the bottom is **Bottoming Out**, suggesting gentle, stabilizing actions like getting comfortable, drinking water, listening to music, or doing Lion’s Breath. Three layered green arrows curve downward, showing the momentum of the slump and how different points of intervention can change the steepness of the fall.

# Clear Light Mode (Be, Neither/All)
The infographic presents a large, multi-column map showing how every phase of the Archetypal Wavelength expresses both its healthy “Rx” qualities and its excessive “OD” qualities. The title reads “BE (Neither/All),” with a subtitle explaining that this mode involves “Surfing the Wavelength through all Phases and all Stages.”

Six vertical panels represent the phases: **Rising**, **Peaking**, **Withdrawal**, **Diminishing**, **Bottoming Out**, and **Restoration**. Each panel is split into two lists—one for balanced expressions (Rx) and one for overdriven expressions (OD). In Rising, the Rx list includes qualities like commitment, inspiration, productivity, and connection; the OD list includes overcommitment, grandiosity, domination, and delusion. Peaking shows Rx qualities such as diligence, joy, attunement, belonging, and gnosis, with OD expressions like ecstasy turned manic, power-over, crusade energy, megalomania, and chaos. Withdrawal’s Rx qualities include steadiness, introspection, discernment, and receptive retreat, while its OD column lists burnout, anxiety, self-medication, paranoia, and obsessive detail-tracking. Diminishing Rx includes security, tranquility, self-acceptance, and unwinding; its OD expresses grasping, shame, rage, alienation, and confusion. Bottoming Out’s Rx column contains convalescence, surrender, repose, and metabolizing experience; its OD column describes overwhelm, self-loathing, subjugation, despair, and isolation. The Restoration column shows Rx qualities such as recuperation, vulnerability, assembling insights, and pattern-seeking, while OD expressions include self-repression, bitterness, presumption, revenge fantasy, and rigid belief salience.

Soft gray arcs trace the downward and upward sweep of the Wavelength across all six panels, showing how the Clear Light mode perceives the entire cycle at once, holding each phase without preference or resistance.

# Clear Light Practice (Staying on the same practice despite the wave)

The infographic shows the Archetypal Wavelength applied to the act of practice itself. The title reads “PRACTICE ITSELF,” with the subtitle “Obstacles ARE the Path.” A gray downward-and-upward arc represents the full cycle.

The cycle begins at **Rising**, labeled “1,” where practice is consistent, diligent, and deliberate. It moves to **Peaking**, “2,” which describes a balanced integration of one or more APTITUDE stages. At **Withdrawal**, “3,” a sudden life event or “karma-generated emergency” temporarily derails practice. The curve continues into **Diminishing**, “4,” where the benefits of practice fade after a delay. Next is **Bottoming Out**, “5,” marked by despair about ever returning to the previously attained state. Finally the arc turns upward into **Restoration**, “6,” where the practitioner returns to practice—often by switching techniques or re-engaging in a new way. Two curved arrows illustrate the descent and the ascent, emphasizing that setbacks are woven directly into the path of growth.
